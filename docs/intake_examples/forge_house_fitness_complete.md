Client Name: Forge House Fitness Kuwait
Industry: Fitness / Premium Gym / Performance Training

Business Summary:
Forge House Fitness Kuwait is a premium training club in Kuwait focused on strength training,
body recomposition, personal training, small-group coaching, and high-performance habits.
The brand is serious, driven, disciplined, and premium, but never arrogant.

Target Audience:
Primary audience is men and women ages 21-38 in Kuwait who care about looking strong,
feeling sharp, and joining a disciplined gym community. They are office professionals,
young entrepreneurs, university graduates, and image-conscious adults who want visible results.

Services / Offers:
- Premium gym memberships
- Personal training
- Small-group strength classes
- Body recomposition coaching
- Nutrition accountability check-ins
- Transformation packages

Brand Voice Tone:
confident, disciplined, premium, motivating, sharp

Brand Voice Style:
Direct, clean, performance-driven copy. Short strong hooks. No cheesy inspiration.
The brand should sound like a serious coach who respects effort, discipline, and visible results.

Dialect Notes:
Primary output can stay in Gulf-market friendly wording. Avoid childish slang. Keep it premium,
masculine/feminine balanced, and socially aspirational.

Brand Identity:
Forge House is not a cheap neighborhood gym. It sells structure, identity, performance, and
physical presence. Members should feel that joining Forge House means entering a stronger,
more disciplined version of themselves.

Brand Voice Examples (3-5 required):
1. Results do not come from mood. They come from structure, repetition, and standards.
2. Train with intent. Build a physique that speaks before you do.
3. This is where discipline becomes visible.
4. Serious training. Serious environment. Serious change.
5. If you want comfort, stay home. If you want progress, come train.

Do / Avoid Rules:
- Do sound premium, sharp, and performance-driven.
- Do emphasize discipline, consistency, confidence, and transformation.
- Do use strong opening hooks that make effort feel aspirational.
- Avoid cheesy motivation quotes.
- Avoid fake hype, clownish slang, or childish internet language.
- Avoid talking like a discount gym.
